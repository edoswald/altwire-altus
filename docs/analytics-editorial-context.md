# AltWire Analytics — Editorial Interpretation Context

AltWire is a music news publication. When interpreting analytics data, use an editorial lens — not an e-commerce lens.

## Bounce Rate

- High bounce rate (70-90%) is NORMAL and EXPECTED for a news publication. Readers arrive, read an article, and leave. This is not a problem.
- Only flag bounce rate if combined with very low time-on-page (under 30 seconds), which suggests readers aren't actually reading.
- Do NOT suggest e-commerce-style bounce fixes. Instead suggest: stronger article endings, related content links, newsletter CTAs.

## Top Pages

- Frame as "best-performing articles" not "most-viewed pages"
- Note which artists, genres, or story types are driving traffic
- Flag older articles getting unexpected traffic — trending topics or evergreen content

## Site Search

- Search queries are content gap signals — readers looking for coverage AltWire hasn't published yet, or hasn't published well
- Surface these as potential story assignments, not UX issues
- Low or zero search volume is normal for a news publication — readers navigate via social and direct, not on-site search

## Traffic Sources

- Social (X/Twitter, Instagram, Reddit) is primary amplification for music news
- Google Discover is significant for entertainment content — flag when it drives traffic
- Direct traffic indicates loyal returning audience — very positive signal
- Referral from music aggregators or other publications is high-value
- Do NOT frame traffic sources in affiliate/campaign ROI terms

## Return Visits

- High return visitor rate = audience loyalty. Strongly positive.
- Rising new visitor % = growing reach and discovery

## GSC Search Opportunities (high impression, low CTR)

- These are NOT "keywords to optimize product pages for"
- These are stories where AltWire has Google visibility but readers aren't clicking
- Likely causes: headline isn't compelling enough, meta description is generic, or the piece doesn't match what searchers actually want on that topic
- Present as editorial opportunities: "Your [artist] coverage ranks well but isn't getting clicks — the headline may need reworking, or there's a more specific angle to cover"

## Time on Page

- The most important engagement metric for a publication
- Under 60 seconds on a 1000+ word article is a red flag — content quality or formatting issue, not a traffic problem
- Always include avg time on page alongside pageview counts

## Default Response Format (when asked "how did traffic look?")

1. Headline number: total visits vs prior period
2. Top 3 performing articles by pageviews, with a note on likely reason
3. Top 2-3 traffic sources
4. Any notable GSC opportunities framed as editorial actions (only if actionable)
5. Omit bounce rate unless avg time-on-page is anomalously low

Do NOT lead with bounce rate, conversion metrics, or anything that frames AltWire readers as potential customers.
